I think it is incredibly important to build a portfolio of your game development works! You will be building out your final 1-\/page website (it can be all html and simple) to market your final project (Note\+: You could re-\/use this template for your next project, and potentially other personal projects).

The following are the requirements for a 1-\/page .html website.


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item Provide a 2-\/3 minute video trailer (preferably a link or embedded You\+Tube Video) followed by at least 3 screenshots of your game (order matters, video first, then screenshots below)
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Your video should highlight the data-\/driven nature of your final project (show your engine and tools in use alongside the game)
\item Your video should otherwise include your \textquotesingle{}narration\textquotesingle{} of your project (i.\+e. I should hear one or more of your teammates talking, not just music playing).
\item You do not need to keep the 30-\/second progress video from the beta on this website unless you\textquotesingle{}d like to move it to the very end.
\end{DoxyItemize}
\item Your documentation (i.\+e. a link to your doxygen generated files)
\item {\bfseries{An image}} of your engine architecture.
\item A link to your binary
\item A short post mortem (i.\+e. A few paragraphs describing how you would take the project further, what went well, and what you would change if given another month on the project) should be put together on a 1-\/page .html page.
\item {\bfseries{DO not}} make the course staff install \textquotesingle{}npm\textquotesingle{} and run your website locally -- just provide a simple link to a one-\/page website that is hosted online (or otherwise local html page that can be downloaded).
\item If you are hosting your website online (which I encourage), make sure to visit your link (e.\+g. Incognito/private mode) to make sure that I will also be able to visit the website and have access to all links provided.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
